TIN MAN

Aside from being the resident mascot, the Tin Man is also an embodiment of one of the school's philosophies, "Technology with a Heart".
As the story goes, the Tin Man is capable of doing almost anything that requires strength and intellect. However, one thing was missing from him: a heart—which he eventually found when he came to FAITH.

MATER FIDELIUM

The Mater Fidelium's exquisite image was inspired by Madonna and Child figure formed by Robert Brumby and is currently located at Liverpool England. It is a work by Vox Angelus Bustinera and a modified concept for Philippine and FAITH settings.

TEMPUS

Juan Sajid Imao's towering creation functions as a sundial to remind students: the value of time. It highlights punctuality, teaching learners to respect not only their time but others' as well. Ultimately, efficiently managing one's time leads to a productive and successful life.

ON BECOMING A BETTER FIRST ASIAN

THE TEMPUS

The three young persons on the Tempus symbolizes both the three great Christian virtues and FAITH's three core values. When implemented, it would produce a first Asian who faithfully believes in oneself and God, showing competence in his future ventures. Who shows commitment in any challenge, boasting a spirit full of hope; and perhaps most importantly, a student who gives love and charity - he who possesses a compassionate heart.

— ven

THE MATER FIDELIUM

Mater Fidelium means "Mother of the Faithful" and specifically chosen by the FAITH Colleges for it to be a reminder for the students and its people. The school wanted to show that they are fully alive, welcoming, and accommodating to the people and the community. This just shows how a respected school like FAITH Colleges teaches its students to be vigorous and complaisant to the community.
