THE FAITH ANECDOTAL HISTORY

Prior to his endeavor in running an educational institution, Mr. Saturnino G. Belen Jr. managed businesses in educational publishing, electronics, freight forwarding, and among others under First Asia Venture Capital Inc., a venture capital company he founded in 1983. The educational publishing businesses under his venture capital are DIWA and Bato Balani Foundation Inc.
Fastech, the electronics company under this group won the Golden Shell Awards for Excellence in Exports twice during the Ramos administration. This prestigious award is given to outstanding Filipino corporations.
Aside from being Fastech's Chairman and CEO, Mr. Belen is also an officer of the Semiconductor and Electronics Industries in the Philippines, Inc. (SEIPI), the leading organization of local and foreign semiconductor and electronics companies in the country.

As part of the accompanying business delegation, he was able to join state and working visits overseas of then President Fidel V. Ramos.
Mr. Belen was also invited by Ramos on a trip to Israel, where the former president would receive an accolade from the Ben-Gurion University at Negev, a desert at Israel.

The inspiration from the university at the desert, along with his strong belief that only education can improve people's lives motivated Mr. Belen to challenge the inadequacy he saw in the education system in the Philippines. Thus, he decided to invest his time and his money in a school.
After his deal in acquiring a school in Tanauan, Batangas did not push through, Mr. Belen and his team decided to build one from scratch.

With his plans of building a future university, which would require at least a 5-hectare campus, he partnered with some local residents to build the present campus.

As the founder of the venture capital company, First Asia, Mr. Belen came up with adding these two words to an institution that integrates Technology and Humanities as the foundation of the education. With First Asia Venture Capital Inc.'s core values of Competence, Commitment and Consistency, an educational institution that is grounded on Christian humanistic values, the consistency is replaced with Compassion.
Finally, First Asia Institute of Technology and Humanities has been established on the 8th day of September 2000 as an institution with the core values of Competence, Commitment and Compassion, which is also organized to continuously strive for excellence.
